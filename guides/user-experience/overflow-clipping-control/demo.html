Overflow Clipping Control

Notice: Your browser does not support overflow: clip. Using overflow: hidden.
Notice: Your browser does not support overflow-clip-margin. CSS Fallback (clip-path) active.

This demo uses a high-contrast test image to clearly show where clipping occurs. The blue dashed line is the content box edge, and the red dashed line is the padding box edge. For non-replaced elements (like the container <div>), declaring overflow: clip is mandatory for overflow-clip-margin to work.

content-box

Clips content strictly at the content box boundary (blue line). The padding area remains white.

padding-box

Clips content at the padding box boundary (red line). The image fills the padding area but stops at the border.

border-box

Clips content at the outer edge of the border. You might see the image bleed into the border area if the border has some transparency or if we used a thinner border, but here it stops at the outer edge.

20px (Length)

Clips content 20px outside the padding box edge. The image extends 20px into the border and beyond.

content-box 15px

Clips content 15px outside the content box edge (halfway through the 30px padding).
