## Best Practices

Even a 0.1-second improvement in key site speed metrics can lead to significant increases in progression rates, conversion rates, and average order value. Focusing on improving metrics like First Meaningful Paint (or Largest Contentful Paint as its successor), Estimated Input Latency, Average Page Load Time, and Time To First Byte can have a substantial impact on user experience and business outcomes.

**Key Metrics to Optimize:**
- **First Meaningful Paint (FMP):** Time until the user sees the primary content on the page. (Note: FMP is deprecated, use **Largest Contentful Paint (LCP)** instead.)
- **Estimated Input Latency:** The time it takes for the browser to respond to user input.
- **Observed Load (Avg. Page Load Time):** The total time it takes for a page to load.
- **Max Server Latency (Time To First Byte - TTFB):** The time from the browser requesting a page to receiving the first byte of information from the server.

Ensure improvements are made across all key metrics by at least 0.1 seconds to observe positive impacts.

## Impact by Vertical

Optimizing mobile site speed can yield different but significant benefits across various industries:

### Retail

- **Progression Rates:**
    - Product Listing Page to Product Detail Page: 3.2% increase
    - Product Details Page to Add To Basket Page: 9.1% increase
- **Average Order Value:** 9.2% increase

### Luxury

- **Progression Rates:**
    - Contact Us Page: 20.6% increase
    - Product Detail Page to Add To Basket Page: 40.1% increase
- **Session Duration:** Longer sessions recorded throughout the browsing journey.

### Travel

- **Check-out Completion:** 2.2% increase
- **Booking Rates:** 10% improvement

### Lead Generation

- **Progression to Form Submission Page:** 21.6% improvement
- **Page Views:** 7% increase

## About the Study

The "Milliseconds Make Millions" study, commissioned by Google and conducted by 55 and Deloitte, investigated the impact of mobile site speed on brand revenue. The research analyzed 37 leading European and American brand sites, collecting data from over 30 million user sessions. Rigorous auditing of tracking and analytics setups was performed, with no UX redesigns occurring during the study period. Mobile load times were monitored for 30 days at the end of 2019, and results were consolidated against revenue-related metrics.
